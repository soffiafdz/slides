Background and Related Work

The hippocampus

  • Located in medial temporal lobe
  • Critical for memory and spatial navigation
    Andersen et al., 2009
  • Vulnerable to aging and pathology
Essential brain structure located in the medial temporal lobe. Key roles: Memory consolidation, spatial navigation, learning. Highly susceptible to aging and neurodegeneration. Structural MRI measures its volume, shape, and integrity. Used as biomarker for structural integrity and cognitive health

Hippocampal atrophy across aging

  • Gradual volume loss during normal aging Fjell et al., 2010
  • Accelerated atrophy → pathological aging Jack et al., 2018
Early detection of pathological decline requires clear normative data. Wide variation even in normal aging complicates clinical assessment.

Alzheimer's disease

The neuropathology of AD is characterized by:
(1) deposition and accumulation of β-amyloid (Aβ) peptides, or amyloid/senile plaques between the neuronal extracellular space,
-Starts basal portions of cortex on the medial-temporal-lobe;
-Asocciation areas;
-All areas including sensory and motor core fields.
(2) formation of neuronal intracellular neurofibrillary tangles of hyperphosphorylated tau protein,
-Braak Stages I,II: transentorhinal
-Braak Stages III,IV: limbic
-Braak Stages V,VI: isocortical
(3) neuronal loss, and (4) neuroinflammation (Miziak et al., 2021).

Hippocampal volume is one of the late but crucial MRI markers in this cascade! The exact proportion is dependent on age and genetic background of the study cohort, but ranges from approximately 20 to 40% (Sperling et al., 2011).

Normative modeling

  • Helps interpret individual atrophy in reference to population norms
  • Captures nonlinear and age-related trends
  • Enables Z-scores relative to age and sex norms
"Normative modeling offers an alternative: instead of controlling for ICV, it learns expected trajectories based on age and sex [Marquand et al., 2019]." "Deviation from this model gives us Z-scores — individual-level indices of abnormality."

Hippocampal normative values

"Nobis et al. (2019) created hippocampal percentile charts, showing how volume varies with age and sex."
"They found high inter-individual variability, with some youth having HC volumes similar to older adults."
Using the data of 19,793 participants in the UK Biobank to extract normative values for hippocampal and total gray matter volume as a function of age (Nobis et al., 2019) Trajectory of hippocampal volume with age.

Limitations of normative Z-scores

  • Z-scores rely on accurate, unbiased population norms
  • Can still reflect residual confounds (e.g., ICV, sex)
  • May not generalize across samples or segmentation pipelines
Z-scores are valuable but depend on the assumption that the normative model is properly specified. Even well-modeled Z-scores can retain bias if variables like ICV or sex were inadequately modeled or if the segmentation pipeline skews volume estimates. Additionally, applying normative models to other datasets may reduce validity due to scanner effects, site differences, or demographic shifts.

Hippocampal-to-Ventricle Ratio

  • Captures relative MTL integrity accounting for surrounding atrophy
  • Reduces dependence on head-size normalization
  • Enhances sensitivity to age-related neurodegeneration
"But even biologically motivated indices like HVR benefit from being embedded in models that account for complex multivariate structure — such as latent cognition and indirect effects."

The HVR offers a simple yet biologically grounded alternative — contextualizing hippocampal atrophy with its surrounding ventricular space.
Because both structures are affected by neurodegeneration, the ratio can offer a more stable and sensitive index of MTL integrity.
HVR showed significantly stronger negative associations with age and was a better predictor of memory compared to standard hippocampal volume

Structural Equation Modeling (SEM)

  • Cognitive function:
    multidimensional & highly intercorrelated
  • Simple regression may fail to account for these complexities
  • (SEM) allows:
    • Latent variable modeling
    • Simultaneous estimations
Cognitive functioning is complex — domains like memory, executive function, and processing speed overlap. Instead of treating each measure separately, SEM lets us model latent constructs that capture shared variance. Crucially, SEM allows us to evaluate associations with imaging metrics like HVR while accounting for intercorrelations across cognitive domains — something simple regressions can’t handle well.

Research Problem
& Aim

Research Problem

  • The hippocampus (HC) is a widely used structural MRI biomarker for aging and neurodegeneration
  • Yet, HC volume shows high variability even in healthy populations
  • Sex differences in HC are debated, often confounded by head-size normalization strategy
  • Interpretation of HC volume alone can be ambiguous across the lifespan or clinical groups

Research Problem

  • The hippocampal-to-ventricle ratio (HVR) was proposed as an alternative biomarker
    • It was proposed on a relatively small sample of healthy older adults
    • It's manual calculation prohibits its use on large samples

Overall Research Aim

To ease, validate, and characterize the use of hippocampal and ventricular biomarkers—particularly the hippocampal-to-ventricle ratio (HVR)—for improved understanding of sex differences and cognitive decline in both aging and neurodegeneration.

Objectives
& Hypotheses

General Objectives

  • Develop tools to ease the application and quality control of the segmentations needed for the calculation of the HVR
  • Validate and expand the preliminary results of HVR on a larger and clinical sample
  • Build normative models of HVR for its use in the clinic
  • Explore and compare HVR with other ways of controlling for head-size in medio-temporal volumetrics
  • Test whether including ventricular data improves models linking brain structure to cognitive performance

Hypotheses

  • The process of data curation and inspection can be made faster and more robust with the use of collaborative software
  • Novel CNN-based segmentations yield more accurate and robust HVR estimates than older methods
  • HVR shows stronger associations with aging and cognition than conventional HC volume
  • Normative models using HVR are more sensitive to age effects than those using conventional HC volume
  • Models including both HC and ventricular volumes better predict cognition than those with HC alone

Non-linear Aging Trajectories

HC, HVR, and lateral ventricles

  • We used GAMLSS to model the non-linear aging trajectories of HC, HVR and the temporal horns of the lateral ventricles
  • From these models, we derived:
    • Z-scores to assess individual deviations.
    • Normative tables across the later lifespan.
Generalized Additive Models for Location, Scale, and Shape (GAMLSS): Chosen for flexibility in modelling nonlinear age effects and non-normal distributions. Box-Cox Cole & Green (BCCG) were selected over Normal distributions by comparing AIC/BIC. Measures modeled: Hippocampal volume (Unadjusted, Proportions method, and Residuals method) Lateral ventricular volume (Unadjusted, Proportions method, and Residuals method) HVR (Residuals method) Stratified by Sex. Predictors: Age (modeled as smooth, nonlinear effect); Sex & Education as covariates. TIV was also added as covariate only on the unadjusted volumes. Outcomes: Age- and sex-adjusted normative centiles (10th to 90th, intervals of 5) Individualized Z-scores using model-derived parameters

Age-related patterns vary by marker

  • HC: modest age related decline; roughly linear
  • Lateral ventricles: clear increase with age
  • HVR:narrower percentiles in early age
    Captures imabalance between HC loss and ventricular expansion
Hippocampal Volume (HC) plot: The centile bands (p10–p90) remain fairly consistently wide across the lifespan. Variability is already present in mid-adulthood (~50s–60s). Suggests early inter-individual differences that may reflect both brain size and segmentation noise—even after ICV correction. HVR plot: Noticeably narrower centile spread (especially in the 50–65 age range). Tighter clustering around the median (p50), especially in females. Variability increases more sharply after age 70, especially in males. Interpretation: HVR shows reduced mid-life variability → suggests it is less influenced by residual anatomical confounds (e.g., head size, segmentation biases). The increase in variability in older age likely reflects pathological divergence, not early noise. This supports the idea that HVR "normalizes" anatomical variability and enhances sensitivity to deviation that truly reflects neurodegeneration—rather than background anatomical variation.

Age-related patterns vary by sex

  • Residual sex effects remain even after covariate adjustment
  • Males consistently show larger ventricular volumes, with wider centiles
  • Males have more pronounced divergence in both lateral ventricles & HVR

Persistent sex differences

  • Matching males and females on ICV minimized volume differences in raw HC
  • Proportional and residual adjustments reduced or reversed sex effects
  • HVR consistently preserved modest but meaningful sex differences
  • Ventricular volume retained sex effects, independently of adjustment
  • Suggests that some sex differences in brain structure are not explained by head size.
  • HVR may capture these residual biological differences more faithfully than conventional corrections

What about cognition?

  • We used the derived Z-scores to quantify the individual deviations from age-normed HC and HVR trajectories
  • We tested how HC & HVR related differently to cognition

Maximum Likelihood (ML) estimation Standard errors and 95% confidence intervals calculated with bootstrap 10k iterations OLS Regression estimates the relationship between a set of predictors (independent variables) and a single outcome (dependent variable) by minimizing the sum of squared differences between observed and predicted values (least squares). It’s a single equation, all variables are observed, and assumes predictors are measured without error. SEM can: Handle Multiple Equations: Model several dependent variables at once. Incorporate Latent Variables: Include factors not directly measured (e.g., “cognition” as a latent variable measured by several cognitive tests). Account for Measurement Error: Estimates relationships taking into account error in observed variables. Model Indirect Effects and Mediation: SEM can estimate direct and indirect (mediated) pathways, something not possible in a single OLS regression. Use Full Covariance Matrix: SEM uses all relationships (variances/covariances) among variables, not just means.

Models' Fit

“Here we compare several adjustment strategies. The HVR model, with or without additional TIV adjustment, achieves fit indices comparable to traditional covariate and residuals approaches, suggesting it’s a robust alternative for capturing disease-related brain changes. The proportions method, though strong in some indices, may introduce model misfit. Overall, these findings highlight the value of including both hippocampal and ventricular information—either as covariates or through their ratio—to model neurodegeneration more accurately.”

SEM: Summary of findings

  • All models were structurally identical—only HC input differed
  • Proportions-adjusted models were the less reliable
  • HVR consistently showed stronger associations with cognition
  • Adding TIV as a covariate flipped the sex-effects
  • AIC/BIC favoured the full models (HC + TIV + LV)
  • CFI/TLI favoured HVR models.

Chapter 3 Summary — Structure, Adjustment & Interpretation

  • Normative modeling showed HVR & LV better captured age-related structural changes than HC
  • Matched-sample results confirmed that HVR preserves sex effects not explained by head-size
  • Z-scores analyses showed stronger cognitive associations for HC; yet they were largely driven by head-size
  • SEM comparisons showed head-size correction strategy alters interpretability
  • Across analyses, HVR proved sensitive to age, sex-effects, structurally meaningful and less confounded by head-size.
